There is no need for tickets to see a parade...all of them are FREE!! That is why it is called "The Greatest Free Show on Earth! . You only get tickets if you want to sit down on stands as the parades pass.

Mardi Gras is a festival traditionally held in many parts of the world, most notably in New Orleans, Louisiana. It is celebrated on the Tuesday before Ash Wednesday, which marks the beginning of the Christian season of Lent.

The origins of Mardi Gras can be traced back to ancient Roman festivals such as Saturnalia and Lupercalia, which were celebrated in mid-February. The tradition was brought to North America by French settlers, and Mardi Gras has been celebrated in New Orleans since the early 1700s.

The colors meaning is that purple represents justice, green represents faith, and gold represents power. The colors selected by Rex, the King of Carnival.

Mardi Gras can be family-friendly events the parades, are generally considered family-friendly, while others, such as the Bourbon Street party scene, are more adult-oriented.

Economic impact reports indicate that Mardi Gras generates over $1 billion in annual spending.
