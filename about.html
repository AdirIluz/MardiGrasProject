Mardi Gras:

Mardi Gras is a festive season that culminates on Fat Tuesday, which is the day before Ash Wednesday and the beginning of the Christian season of Lent. Mardi Gras celebrations are held around the world, but New Orleans is perhaps the most famous and well-known location for these festivities. In New Orleans, Mardi Gras is marked by elaborate parades, colorful costumes, and lively music. The celebrations typically begin in early January and continue through Fat Tuesday, which falls on a different date each year depending on the timing of Easter. The parades are the main attraction of Mardi Gras in New Orleans. There are dozens of parades throughout the season, each featuring large floats, marching bands, and costumed revelers throwing beads and other trinkets to the crowds. The most well-known parades are held on the final weekend of the season, including the Bacchus and Endymion parades. Mardi Gras in New Orleans is a time of celebration and excess, and it attracts visitors from all over the world. However, it's important to note that the celebrations can also be crowded and rowdy, and visitors should take care to stay safe and respectful while enjoying the festivities.

LoToya Centrell

LaToya Cantrell was born on December 3, 1972, in Los Angeles, California, and later moved to New Orleans. She was elected as the mayor of New Orleans on November 18, 2017, and assumed office on May 7, 2018. She is the first woman to hold the office of mayor in New Orleans' 300-year history, as mayor, LaToya Cantrell has been responsible for overseeing the city's preparations for the festivities. However, due to the COVID-19 pandemic, Mardi Gras celebrations in 2021 and 2022 were significantly scaled back to prevent the spread of the virus.
In 2021, Cantrell issued an executive order banning all parades and other large gatherings, citing concerns over the potential for COVID-19 transmission. However, she did allow for "house floats," where residents decorated their homes in Mardi Gras-themed displays, as a safe alternative to the traditional parades. In 2022, Cantrell similarly limited Mardi Gras celebrations due to the ongoing pandemic, but did allow some smaller parades to take place under strict safety protocols. These parades were limited in size and route, and all participants were required to wear masks and practice social distancing.
